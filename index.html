üîß Technical Implementation

NATO-style visualization using weighted Voronoi Treemap with continental grouping.

üåç Four-Continent Grouping Strategy:

  • Why 4 continents instead of 7? Creates cleaner, more distinct regional clusters similar to NATO defense spending visualization
  • Selected regions:
    • Asia - 10 most populous countries (China, India, Indonesia, etc.)
    • Americas - North + South America (USA, Brazil, Mexico, etc.)
    • Africa - 5 major population centers (Nigeria, Ethiopia, Egypt, etc.)
    • Europe - Including Russia (Russia, Germany, UK, France, etc.)
  • Data structure: Two-level hierarchy - continents as parent nodes, countries as leaf nodes

üéØ NATO-Style Effect Implementation:

  • Step 1 - Continental Voronoi: Calculate 4 large continent polygons using weighted treemap
    • Each continent gets area proportional to its total population
    • Creates distinct regional boundaries (like NATO's alliance groupings)
  • Step 2 - Nested Country Distribution: Within each continent polygon, distribute countries
    • Countries only appear within their parent continent's boundary
    • Maintains visual grouping - all Asian countries cluster together
    • Similar to how NATO chart groups members by region
  • Step 3 - Continent Borders: Draw thick borders (#333, 3px) around continent polygons
    • Creates clear visual separation between continental regions
    • Labels placed outside circle using polygon centroids

üîë Key Difference from Standard Approach:

  • ‚ùå Standard Voronoi (d3.Delaunay): Flat structure, countries distributed by seed point distance only
    • No weight consideration ‚Üí incorrect proportions
    • No grouping ‚Üí countries scatter randomly
    • Result: Fragmented, no regional patterns
  • ‚úÖ Weighted Voronoi Treemap: Hierarchical structure with area-proportional distribution
    • Respects parent-child hierarchy ‚Üí natural grouping
    • Iterative adjustment ‚Üí accurate area matching
    • Result: Clean regional clusters (NATO-style)

üêõ Critical Bug Fixes:

  1. Area proportionality failure:
    • Symptom: China (1.4B) smaller than Iran (84M)
    • Cause: Used d3.Delaunay which ignores weights
    • Evidence from logs: {"countryName":"China","expectedRatio":"35.7%","actualArea":"8.6%"}
    • Fix: Replaced with d3.voronoiTreemap()
    • Result: China now correctly occupies ~24.5% of total area
  2. Continent labels positioned at origin (0,0):
    • Symptom: All 4 continent labels stuck in top-left corner
    • Cause: voronoiTreemap() doesn't preserve continent.x/y from pack layout
    • Evidence from logs: {"x":undefined,"y":undefined,"angle":null,"labelX":null}
    • Fix: Changed from Math.atan2(continent.y - radius, continent.x - radius) to Math.atan2(centroid[1], centroid[0]) where centroid = d3.polygonCentroid(continent.polygon)
    • Result: Labels correctly distributed around circle perimeter
